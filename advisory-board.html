External Advisory Board

Independent experts from across medicine, technology, health policy, and community health who provide strategic guidance to PITTCARE — through annual direct engagement with the center director and participation in the center's annual summer meeting.

How the board works

Guidance through relationship, not just governance

PITTCARE's External Advisory Board is structured around two simple commitments: a personal conversation each year and a collective gathering each summer. No committees, no bureaucracy — just sustained, expert perspective from people who care about getting Intelligence-Technology in primary care right.

01
Annual 1:1 conversation

Each advisory board member meets individually with the PITTCARE center director once per year. These conversations are deliberately open — a chance to share perspective, raise concerns, and offer guidance outside of any formal agenda. The relationship is the mechanism.

02
Annual summer meeting

The full board convenes at the PITTCARE annual meeting each summer. Members hear from center researchers, engage with the Voice of the People Survey findings, and provide collective guidance on the center's research direction, priorities, and emerging questions.

03
Independence by design

Advisory board members are external to the University of Pittsburgh. Their value comes from their distance as much as their expertise — they can say what internal colleagues cannot, and they bring perspectives from clinical practice, policy, industry, and community that the center's research must ultimately serve.

Annual event

PITTCARE Annual Meeting

Held each summer, the PITTCARE annual meeting brings together the external advisory board, research faculty, collaborators, and invited guests for a day of presentation, discussion, and strategic reflection.

The meeting serves as the center's most visible accountability moment — where the Voice of the People Survey findings are presented, active research is shared, and the advisory board offers its collective guidance on what the center should prioritize in the year ahead.

Frequency

Annually, each summer. Advisory board members are expected to attend or participate remotely.

Format

Research presentations, Voice of the People Survey report, open discussion, and closed advisory session with the center director and board members.

Who attends

External advisory board, PITTCARE research faculty and staff, collaborators from Pitt Health Sciences and partner institutions, and invited guests from the broader primary care and IT community.
